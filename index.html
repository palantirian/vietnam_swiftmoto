Country Overview

Vietnam at a Glance

Vietnam (~100M people) is a fast-growing, urbanizing economy with a young, digital-first population. The United States and Vietnam now have a comprehensive strategic partnership, and foreign direct investment continues to flow into manufacturing, technology, and services.

CAGE Summary

  • Cultural: Relationship-driven, high-context, respect for hierarchy.
  • Administrative: Pro-investment but procedures can be complex; use local counsel.
  • Geographic: Time-zone/distance from the United States; regionally central in the Association of Southeast Asian Nations.
  • Economic: Lower costs, rising middle class, rapid e-commerce growth.

Why Ho Chi Minh City first?

  • Largest consumer market & startup hub.
  • Dense districts enable two-hour/same-day delivery promises.
  • Deep merchant base (marketplaces, restaurants, pharmacies, small/medium businesses).

Legal & Incorporation

Setting Up a Vietnamese Firm

Entity & Ownership

  • Limited Liability Company is typical; single or multi-member.
  • Courier/parcel allows up to 100% foreign ownership.
  • Road freight may cap foreign equity at 51% (consider partners).

Licensing Flow

  1. Investment Registration Certificate
  2. Enterprise Registration Certificate
  3. Seal, tax code, bank, payroll & insurance setup

Capital & People

  • No fixed minimum capital; propose realistic charter capital.
  • Appoint a resident Legal Representative.
  • Register business lines: courier/logistics, warehousing, fulfillment.
  • Insurance & liability limits in logistics contracts.

Taxes & Finance

What to Budget

Company Taxes

  • Corporate income tax: 20% standard; 15–17% for qualifying small firms.
  • Value-added tax: 10% typical for services.
  • No United States–Vietnam double taxation agreement yet → use foreign tax credits.

Payroll

  • Employer ≈ Social Insurance, Health Insurance, and Unemployment Insurance contributions for locals; foreigners similar (no Unemployment Insurance).
  • 13th-month customary; personal income tax withheld progressively.

Currency & Repatriation

  • Operate in Vietnamese dong; capital and operating accounts.
  • Profit remittance post-audit & tax clearance.
  • Manage exchange-rate risk (Vietnamese dong ↔ United States dollars).

Real Estate

Office, Hubs & Micro-Warehouses

Office

  • Coworking/serviced office to start.
  • Grade B ~US$20–35/m²; Grade A central business district ~US$60/m².
  • 3-month deposit; 2–3 year term; bilingual contracts.

Warehousing

  • City mini-hubs (100–300 m²) for two-hour service level agreement coverage.
  • Peripheral warehouses ~US$4.7–7/m².
  • Shared facilities to stay asset-light.

Business Culture

How to Work Effectively

Etiquette

  • Warm introductions; relationship first.
  • Address seniors first; exchange cards with two hands.
  • “Yes” can mean “I hear you” — confirm alignment.

Management

  • Private feedback; public praise (“face”).
  • Clear standard operating procedures; bilingual communication; visual job aids.
  • Observe Tết; invest in development.

Risks & Opportunities

What to Watch—And Where to Lean In

Key Risks

  • Procedural delays; evolving regulations; uneven enforcement.
  • Traffic & flooding → service level agreement variability.
  • Price competition; thin margins; cash-on-delivery handling.

Tailwinds

  • Double-digit e-commerce growth; young, online consumers.
  • Pro-investment stance; startup programs; infrastructure build-out.
  • Environmental, social, and governance: electric bikes & carbon-neutral fulfillment.
